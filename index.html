Keypad, QR Reader, or Video Intercom: Which Access Control Fits Your Property?

Feb 5, 2026 | Blog
At a Glance
Choosing the Right Access Control Reader for Your Property

CellGate offers a three-tier approach — Good, Better, Best — to match your property's needs to the right reader. All systems connect to TrueCloud Connect for centralized access management and activity logging.

The Digit Reader handles PIN and card access for low-traffic entry points. The Infinity Reader adds QR codes and Bluetooth for moderate visitor flow. The Watchman Video Intercom delivers full video, voice, and directory capabilities for high-traffic properties.

All three readers work over wired internet or cellular LTE and are backed by CellGate's Gold Key Service and dedicated customer support.

Which Access Control Reader Fits Your Property or Specific Entry Point?

Choosing the right access control readers can feel confusing. Properties have different traffic levels overall and can have very different traffic levels per entry point. Visitor needs may vary by entry point. But security expectations are higher than ever. We often hear the question: "Which access control reader is right for my property or at a specific entry point?"

This guide breaks it down using a simple three-tier approach. Good. Better. Best. We compare keypad access control, QR code access control, and video intercom systems. The goal is to help you match your needs to the right solution.

We focus on three proven gate entry systems from CellGate. Each tier supports modern access control and serves a different type of property. All systems connect through our TrueCloud Connect platform for centralized management.

Good: Digit Reader – PIN + Card Access

The Digit 2-in-1 Reader is a practical and cost-effective access control solution for properties or specific entry points with simple security needs. It focuses on reliable entry control without added complexity, making it easy to manage and maintain.

Key Attributes:

  • Combines a backlit keypad with a proximity card reader in a single device.
  • Supports secure DESFire® EV3 credentials.
  • Compatible with standard 26-bit Wiegand systems, so it integrates smoothly with many existing gate entry systems.
  • Automatically logs all access activity and attempts by issued credential in TrueCloud Connect.

Advantages:

  • Provides dependable keypad access control using PIN codes, cards, or fobs.
  • Simple to operate and well-suited for controlled access points where speed and consistency matter.
  • Streamlined design keeps daily management straightforward.

Considerations:

  • Limited to core access functions with minimal visitor management capabilities (PIN Code access for visitors only).
  • Does not support QR code access control, Bluetooth access control, or camera-based features.
  • Requires an active wired internet or cellular connection.

Best for:

Employee-only entrances, service gates, and low-traffic access points where visual verification is not required, and advanced visitor tools are unnecessary.

Better: Infinity Reader – 4-in-1 Multi-Credential

The Infinity 4-in-1 Reader is a flexible access control solution designed for properties or specific entry points that have some visitor flow, but is less likely that it is the primary visitor entrance. It balances convenience and control by supporting multiple entry methods while keeping operations simple for residents and staff.

Key Attributes:

  • Combines PIN Code keypad entry, QR code access control, Bluetooth 5.0 touchless entry, and card credentials in one device.
  • Scans QR Visitor Passes, including temporary, recurring, and single-use passes.
  • Has a built-in keypad for input of PIN code Visitor Passes.
  • Supports DESFire® EV3, HID credentials, and 26-bit Wiegand compatibility.
  • Automatically logs all access activity and attempts by issued credential in TrueCloud Connect.

Advantages:

  • Improves visitor management flow at entry points by allowing guests, contractors, and delivery drivers to enter using QR codes.
  • Access control for residents/employees via Bluetooth touchless entry is enabled through the CellGate mobile app.
  • Reduces gate or door congestion and improves traffic flow at busy entry points.

Considerations:

  • Does not include voice communication features.
  • Visual verification of visitors (video) is not available.
  • Requires an active wired internet or cellular connection.

Best for:

Properties or entry points with moderate visitor traffic that need QR-based Visitor Passes, Bluetooth contactless entry for residents/employees, and flexible credential options without the need for voice or video communication.

Best: Watchman Video Intercom – Full Communication

A multi-family Watchman Video Intercom model is a comprehensive access control and visitor management solution for high-traffic properties. It combines secure entry methods with live communication tools to give residents and staff greater visibility and control.

Key Attributes:

  • Features a (minimum) 7.5-inch touchscreen (model dependent) with a built-in camera and live streaming video.
  • Includes telephone entry with an on-screen directory supporting a minimum of 1,500 listings (model dependent).
  • Supports QR codes, PIN codes & HID card credentials.
  • Automatically captures photos on all access attempts and logs all activity in TrueCloud Connect.

Advantages:

  • Allows residents to see and speak with visitors before granting access.
  • Enhances security through visual verification and real-time communication.
  • Supports large, multi-tenant properties with high visitor volume.

Considerations:

  • Requires an active wired internet or cellular connection.
  • Designed for properties that need advanced visitor communication tools.
  • Watchman does not support resident/employee Bluetooth access control without the addition of a bracketed Halo reader.

Best for:

Multi-family communities, gated neighborhoods, and commercial properties that require video verification, telephone entry, and robust visitor management.

How to Choose the Right Access Control Reader

Choose the Digit Reader if your property or specific entry point has low visitor volume. It works best for employee access and service entrances. It is a solid starting point for keypad access control.

Choose the Infinity Reader if your property manages a moderate amount of visitors who need access at key entry points. QR codes and Bluetooth access control make entry faster. This is a strong fit for modern visitor management needs.

Choose the Watchman Video Intercom if your property needs full visibility. Video and voice communication add another layer of security. This option supports large directories and high traffic – ideal for vehicle gates and front entrances.

CellGate also offers the Halo Reader as another option. The Halo is a streamlined access control reader that supports Bluetooth access control and secure card credentials. It does not include a keypad, QR scanning, or video features, which keeps entry fast and simple. This reader is best suited for properties or entry points that prioritize mobile access and card-based entry, such as private residential communities, controlled parking areas, or staff-only entrances where visitor management is not required.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I upgrade from a Digit to an Infinity reader later?

Yes. Many properties start with the Digit and upgrade as visitor needs grow.

Do QR code Visitor Passes expire?

Yes. QR codes can be set to expire based on time or usage rules.

What is the difference between DESFire® EV3 and HID credentials?

DESFire® EV3 uses advanced encryption. HID credentials are widely supported and compatible with many systems.

Can Watchman work without the internet?

There are two connectivity options: Watchman multi-family models work on either a wired internet or a wireless cellular LTE connection via Verizon or AT&T.
